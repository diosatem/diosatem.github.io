Tours in Cozumel

A brief history...
During the Mayan period (about 250 to 950 ce), Cozumel was a center for both commercial and ceremonial activities, which explains the small archaeological sites that you can see around the island. In the 1500s, a Spaniard named Juan de Grijalva led the first Spanish expedition to Cozumel. From then on, the Spaniards used the island as abase for Spanish efforts to conquer the Maya.

Cozumel has tons of tourist attractions round the island that would surely mesmerize you. From east to west, north to south, the island is a sure treasure to both beach and adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best attractions of the island.

✓ Punta Sur Eco Beach Park
An ecological park that covers the reefs, beaches, lagoons, and low forest of the surrounding area.
✓ El Mirador
You can find kiosks and unique rock formations and great for photography.
✓ Chankanaab Reef
At a depth of 70 feet, this reef is a great spot to learn how to snorkel.
✓ Papito's Beach Club & Restaurant Bar Cozumel
The latest and most exclusive beach club in the island. Sandy beach.
✓ San Gervasio Mayan Archaeological Site
An ancient Mayan ruins. This site is where women paid tribute to the goddess of love and fertility.
✓ Mercado Municipal
Authentic Mexican food, clothing, spices, housewares, and people.
✓ El Cedral
Ancient ruins.
✓ Mr Sanchos Beach Club Cozumel
A restaurant and bar and has the largest private beach on the island. You can enjoy their Aquatic Park, wave runners, parasailing, banana boat rides, snorkeling tours, scuba diving, or you could ride a horse or an ATV through the jungle and the beach.
